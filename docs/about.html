About

p5.gysin is a small p5.js library for generative, plotter-friendly traces. It treats shapes and words as fragile vector material: lines hesitate, repeat, overshoot, break, and sometimes disappear.

Why vector-first?

The library does not inspect canvas pixels after drawing. Every trace starts as a captured vector shape, becomes sampled points, and remains exportable as paths.

Influence

The name points to Brion Gysin, cut-up procedures, concrete poetry, worn print, and machine-made imperfection. The library is not a rough.js clone; it is about vulnerable traces and disappearing words.

Status

Version 0.2.0 adds validated input, deterministic rerolls, physical page export, layers, penmapping, optional route optimization, and plotting statistics to the original vector-first MVP.
